# Speaking and Writing Skills for Web Developers

Communication is as important as code. Whether you are writing a documentation, sending an email to a client, or explaining a bug in a daily stand-up, clear English is essential.

## 1. Writing Skills

Writing clearly ensures your code comments, commit messages, and emails are understood.

### Key Grammar Rules

- **Tenses**: Understand Past, Present, and Future to describe _when_ a bug happened or _when_ a feature will be done.
- **Prepositions**: Use correct positioning words (in, on, at, by).
- **Determiners**: (a, an, the, this, that).
- **Right Forms of Verb**: Matching subject and verb.
- **Order of Adjectives**: e.g., "A big red button" (Size before Color).
- **Conditional Sentences**: "If-else" logic in English (e.g., "If I deploy this, the site might break").
- **Capitalization & Punctuation**: Professional writing demands proper casing and comma usage.
- **Sentence Structures**: Master Simple, Compound, and Complex sentences.

### Professional Email Writing

- **Formats**: Learn standard professional email structures.
- **Common Mistakes**: Avoid slang or overly casual language in formal contexts.
- **How to write an Email**: Clear subject line, polite greeting, concise body, and professional sign-off.

### Proof-reading Tools

Don't rely solely on your brain. Use tools to check your work:

- **Grammarly**: Checks spelling and grammar in real-time.
- **QuillBot**: Helps rephrase awkward sentences.
- **ChatGPT**: Great for generating email drafts or checking tone.
